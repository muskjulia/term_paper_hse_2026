Overview¶

This notebook presents an empirical analysis of Reddit discussions about generative artificial intelligence in video game culture. It is conducted as part of Chapter 3 of the Term Paper titled "Machine learning (artificial intelligence) and modern culture: events and artifacts."

The notebook is built on two data sets gathered from Reddit through automated collection methods and stored in a PostgreSQL relational database. The first data set includes higher-level information, such as metadata from Reddit streams and manually encoded variables. The second data set consists of public reactions at the comment level related to Reddit posts.

Introduction¶

Artificial intelligence is now embedded in all aspects of human life, automating routine tasks and complex computations. This marks the start of an era where AI shapes cultural norms, influences development trends, and provokes diverse reactions from society unprepared for such change.

The video game industry, characterized by its isolated community, offers a fitting context for examining issues related to two-dimensional perception and differing opinions on artificial intelligence. Games, whether completed or still in development, involve large companies or indie studios and blend visual art, storytelling, voice acting, music, and programming.

The purpose of the work¶

The purpose of this term paper is to analyse perceptions of generative AI in video games in discussions on the Reddit platform within specialised communities, and to determine whether people's attitudes towards AI differ depending on their role in the industry.

Research hypotheses¶

The empirical analysis follows the three hypotheses formulated in the term paper:

Asymmetry by professional role (H1): newcomers to the field will be more positive about generative AI than experienced developers.

Asymmetry by type of AI use (H2): the use of artificial intelligence in elements encountered by the player (illustrations, voice acting, and narration) elicits more negative reactions than when used as a tool for internal development.

The community's response to AI discovery depends on the company's integrity (H3): The community reacts when the company voluntarily admits to using AI, since the players do not interpret a cover-up as deception or a betrayal of trust.

Methods¶

The notebook includes data loading, data cleaning, construction of analytical subsets, descriptive statistics, cross-tabulations, chi-square tests, Fisher’s exact tests, Cramér’s V effect size estimation, visualisations, and optional logistic regression as a robustness check.

def frequency_table(series, name):
    table = series.value_counts(dropna=False).rename_axis(name).reset_index(name="n")
    table["%"] = (table["n"] / table["n"].sum() * 100).round(2)
    return table

Part IV: Auxiliary functions for hypothesis testing¶

This section describes reusable functions for cross-tabulation, row percentage calculation, the chi-square test, and Сramer's V. These functions will be used later to test H1, H2, and H3 in the same format.

In [24]:
def crosstab_counts(df, row, col):
    """Сross-tabulation for two categorical variables"""
    data = df.dropna(subset=[row, col]).copy()
    return pd.crosstab(data[row], data[col])

def row_percent_table(table):
    """Сross-tabulation into %."""
    return (table.div(table.sum(axis=1), axis=0) * 100).round(2)

def cramers_v(table):
    """Cramér's V effect size for a contingency table"""
    chi2, p, dof, expected = chi2_contingency(table)
    n = table.to_numpy().sum()
    r, k = table.shape
    
    if min(r - 1, k - 1) == 0:
        return np.nan
    return np.sqrt((chi2 / n) / min(r - 1, k - 1))
    
def association_test(table, label):
    """Chi-square test with a compact summary"""
    chi2, p, dof, expected = chi2_contingency(table)
    min_expected = expected.min()
    v = cramers_v(table)
    
    test_summary = pd.DataFrame({
        "analysis": [label],
        "chi2": [round(chi2, 4)],
        "p_value": [round(p, 6)],
        "dof": [dof],
        "min_expected_count": [round(float(min_expected), 4)],
        "cramers_v": [round(float(v), 4)]
    })
    
    return test_summary
def run_categorical_analysis(df, row, col, label):
    """Display the results: counts, row percentages, and chi-square test"""
    counts = crosstab_counts(df, row, col)
    percentages = row_percent_table(counts)
    summary = association_test(counts, label)
    
    display(Markdown(f"### {label}"))
    display(Markdown("**Counts**"))
    display(counts)
    
    display(Markdown("**Row percentages**"))
    display(percentages)
    
    display(Markdown("**Test summary**"))
    display(summary)
    
    return counts, percentages, summary

Part VI: Asymmetry depending on the type of use of artificial intelligence (H2)¶

H2 predicts that visible AI applications like AI art, voice, gameplay features, character design, narrative content, or promotional materials will provoke more negative reactions than internal AI tools used for coding, prototyping, testing, localisation, or workflow efficiency.

This hypothesis is examined both through publications and comments.

Step 6.1: Creation of subgroups for testing the H2 hypothesis¶

We can categorise AI types into three groups: art, voice, and gameplay. These can be combined into a new variable called visible_ai, while other tools are grouped as internal_tools, and general topics fall under general_discussion.

Grouping by H2 shows that the most relevant posts and comments are about applications with visible AI. Highlighting visible AI's prominence helps the audience see its importance, making them feel their focus on this area is recognised. At the publication level, visible AI accounts for 67.23% of referrals, while internal tools account for 11.32%. Visible AI also dominates at the comment level, accounting for 64.19% of relevant comments, compared with 13.20% for internal tools. This confirms that the dataset is suitable for comparing visible AI applications with internal AI tools.

def run_logit_model(data, formula, label):
    if not statsmodels_available:
        print("statsmodels is not available and logistic regression cannot be estimated.")
        return None, None
    
    model = smf.logit(formula=formula, data=data).fit(disp=False)

    display(Markdown(f"### {label}"))
    print(model.summary())
    
    result_table = pd.DataFrame({
        "term": model.params.index,
        "coef": model.params.values,
        "odds_ratio": np.exp(model.params.values),
        "p_value": model.pvalues.values
    })
    
    result_table["coef"] = result_table["coef"].round(4)
    result_table["odds_ratio"] = result_table["odds_ratio"].round(4)
    result_table["p_value"] = result_table["p_value"].round(6)
    
    display(Markdown("**Compact interpretation table**"))
    display(result_table)
    
    return model, result_table

Conclusion¶

This notebook examined Reddit discussions on generative AI within video game culture, utilizing datasets at both the post and comment levels. The empirical analysis focused on refined analytical subsets, which included 592 relevant posts and 6666 relevant comments.

The results indicate that attitudes towards generative AI vary throughout the dataset. At the publication level, factors such as the author's role, the manner of AI use, and the type of information disclosed are linked to differences in AI positioning. At the comment level, similar patterns emerge concerning the type of AI, though their influence is less pronounced.

H1 was supported. Outsiders tend to rate generative AI more positively than developers do, who are mostly neutral, whereas players exhibit the highest level of negative attitudes. This indicates that a closer involvement in game development correlates with a more cautious or critical view of AI.

H2 mode is supported. Visible AI applications, particularly those related to AI graphics and other user-focused tools, tend to provoke more negative reactions than internal AI tools. This trend is clear both in published content and in comments, with a stronger correlation at the publication level and a weaker one among comments.

The H3 is partially supported. At the publication level, using AI-discovered or retroactively is linked to a more negative stance, whereas voluntarily sharing information usually results in neutral or less negative responses. However, at the comment level, disclosing information—whether discovered or voluntary does not increase the number of negative comments.

Additional reliability checks using binary logistic regression generally support the main conclusions. The presence of visible artificial intelligence raises the chance of a negative assessment, supporting hypothesis H2. Regarding H3, reliability models show a strong effect at higher levels, but do not support the hypothesis at the comment level.

In conclusion, the analysis reveals that Reddit discussions on AI in games are influenced primarily by three factors: who is speaking, how visible AI use is, and how transparent the information sharing appears. Generative AI is not outright rejected; instead, community reactions vary depending on the context of AI usage, the identity of the discussants, and the openness of reporting.
